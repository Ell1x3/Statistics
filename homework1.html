What is Statistics, and why useful for Cybersecurity?Homework 1

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data to make decisions under uncertainty. In cybersecurity, it turns noisy logs (network traffic, login attempts, malware traces) into actionable signals.

  • Anomaly detection: Model “normal” behavior (means, variances, quantiles). Large deviations (high z-scores) flag suspicious events (e.g., off-hours logins, traffic spikes).
  • Risk & probability: Quantify breach likelihoods and expected impact to prioritize controls and patching.
  • Threat classification (ML): Statistical models power phishing filters, malware detectors, and user-behavior analytics.
  • Evaluate defenses: Measure precision/recall, false-positive rate, ROC/AUC to tune IDS/AV thresholds.
  • Crypto & randomness tests: Use frequency/entropy tests to validate key generators and detect weaknesses.
  • Forensics & response: Reconstruct timelines, estimate dwell time, quantify losses with uncertainty bounds.

In short: statistics provides data-driven tools to separate normal vs. abnormal, reduce uncertainty, and improve detection and response.
